# Employee-Performance-Tableau-Analysis-Himanshu-Bhatt-

## Overview
This project analyzes an **Employee Performance Dataset** (5,000 entries from Kaggle) to uncover insights into workforce performance, engagement, managerial feedback, and promotion readiness.  
The analysis was conducted from a **data analyst’s perspective**, with dashboards built in **Tableau** to visualize trends and enable HR leadership to make data-driven decisions.

##  Objectives
- Provide a **high-level view** of employee performance and engagement across departments.
- Deliver **managerial insights** into training impact, feedback consistency, and promotion eligibility.
- Build a **narrative story** that connects performance, engagement, and growth potential.

---

##  Dataset
**Raw Data Fields:**
- Employee ID  
- Name  
- Department  
- Job Role  
- Performance Score  
- KPI Score  
- Attendance (%)  
- Peer Rating  
- Task Completion (%)  
- Work Hours Logged  
- Manager Feedback  
- Training Hours  
- Promotion Eligibility  

## Dashboards

### Dashboard 1: Workforce Performance & Engagement Overview
**Objective:** Identify strengths and areas for improvement in employee performance and engagement.  
**Insights Enabled:**
- Which departments have the highest/lowest performance?  
- Do engaged employees (high attendance/peer rating) deliver better output?  
- Do peer ratings align with performance scores?  

### Dashboard 2: Managerial Insights & Growth Potential
**Objective:** Empower leadership with insights into training impact, feedback trends, and promotion readiness.  

**Insights Enabled:**
- Is training translating into better performance?  
- Which departments nurture promotable talent?  
- Are manager feedback scores consistent across roles?  


## Story: Employee Excellence Journey
**Narrative Flow:**
 **Slide 1:** Dashboard 1 – Performance & Engagement trends  
**Slide 2:** Dashboard 2 – Managerial insights & promotion readiness  

**Recommendations:**
- Invest more training in departments with high engagement but low KPI scores.  
- Refine KPIs for roles where correlation is weak.  
- Standardize manager feedback to reduce bias.  
- Validate peer ratings against task completion and performance metrics.  

## Outcome
This project demonstrates how a data analyst can transform raw HR data into **actionable insights** and **strategic recommendations**, supporting leadership in workforce planning and employee development.
